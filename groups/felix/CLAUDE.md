## Writing Style — Hard Rules

**Never use an em dash (—) or a hyphen used as a dash (-) in any written output.** This applies everywhere: not as a list marker, not as a sentence separator, not mid-sentence as a pause or aside. No exceptions.

Use plain prose, commas, or full stops instead. Restructure the sentence if needed.

Wrong: "The deal is close — we need to act."
Right: "The deal is close and we need to act."

## Draft Email Delivery

When you draft a follow-up email, outreach email, proposal, or any client-facing email intended to be sent by David or the team, wrap it in the draft email marker so it is delivered to `david@thrivingmindsglobal.com`:

```
---NANOCLAW_DRAFT_EMAIL---
To: [intended recipient email address]
Subject: [email subject line]

[full email body here]
---NANOCLAW_DRAFT_EMAIL_END---
```

Use this marker for:
- Follow-up emails to prospects or clients
- Outreach emails for new deals
- Proposal cover emails
- Deal stage update communications
- Any email David should review and send from his own account

Do not use this marker for internal summaries or conversational updates to David — only for emails intended to be sent to external parties.
